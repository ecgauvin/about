Hello, my name is Erika Gauvin, and I currently pursuing my MA/MLIS Digital Humanities at University of Alberta. A little tidbit about me, I identify as a creative artsy person, I have a BFA in ceramics from Georgia State University, and a big crafter, maker of many mediums (paint, drawing, knitting, crocheting, you name it I probably have at least tired it!). Additionally a big part of my identitiy that has driven my artistic practice and research is my cultural heritage. I am Japanese-American and fluent in English and Japanese. Although I have a deep found love and appreciation for my heritage, I also struggle and have immense amount of anger for the Colonialist society these nations were and currently are as a governing body. I understand that I am a single person that can resolve these issues, but I hope through my academic journey of research creation with a critical lense can provide some good to the chaotic place we call Earth.

Which leads me to my final project of Embroidering a Font inspired my traditional Ainu Embroidery, "chijiri." For those unfamiliar, Ainu people are the Indigenous people of Northern Japan. Unfortunately, it is not a unique tale, but the Ainu people were colonized and forced to assimilate into Japanese society, losing much of their culture, heritage and language. They were only officially recognized as Indigenous people of Japan in 2008, which has brought on a cultural awakening, especially the craft of Ainu embroidery. The Ainu believed that certain embroidered patterns and styles could ward off evil spirits, which can be seen with some abstract imagery of chains and thorns. My overarching goal for this project is to figure out a way to expand awareness of Ainu embroidery, and I thought being able to create a typable form of the embroidery would be a great way to do this.
